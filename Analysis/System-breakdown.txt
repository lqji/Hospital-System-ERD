1. Identified Entities

Patient: The individual receiving medical care.

Doctor: The medical professional providing care.

Department: The operational unit within the hospital.

Appointment: The scheduled interaction between a Patient and a Doctor.

Service: The hospital's catalog of available procedures, tests, or treatments.

Medical Record: The clinical documentation resulting from an appointment.

Billing: The financial invoice generated for an appointment.

Payment (Discovered Entity): Required to handle the "partial payment" business logic cleanly.

Appointment_Service (Associative Entity): Required to resolve the Many-to-Many relationship between Appointments and Services, and to track quantities and historical pricing.

2. Identified Attributes


Patient: Patient_ID, First_Name, Last_Name, DOB, Gender, Blood_Group, Contact_Number, Email

Doctor: Doctor_ID, First_Name, Last_Name, Specialization, Is_Active (to handle doctors leaving), Contact_Number

Department: Department_ID, Name, Location

Appointment: Appointment_ID, Appt_Date, Appt_Time, Status (Scheduled/Completed/Cancelled), Type (Consult/Follow-up/Emergency)

Service: Service_ID, Name, Type (Test/Treatment/Surgery), Current_Price

Medical Record: Record_ID, Diagnosis, Treatment_Plan, Prescription, Created_At

Billing: Bill_ID, Total_Amount, Balance_Due, Status (Pending/Partial/Paid), Issued_Date

Payment: Payment_ID, Amount, Payment_Date, Payment_Method

Appointment_Service: Quantity, Billed_Price (locks the catalog price at the time of service)

3. Identified Relationships

Patient to Appointment: A patient can book multiple appointments over time, but a single appointment belongs to exactly one patient.

Doctor to Appointment: A doctor conducts multiple appointments, but a single appointment is handled by exactly one doctor.

Doctor to Department (Employment): A department employs multiple doctors, but a doctor primarily works in one department.

Doctor to Department (Management): A department has one head doctor, and a doctor can head at most one department.

Appointment to Medical Record: A completed appointment generates one medical record.

Appointment to Billing: A completed appointment generates one consolidated bill/invoice.

Billing to Payment: A single bill can receive multiple partial payments until the balance reaches zero.

Appointment to Service: An appointment can include multiple services (e.g., an X-ray and a Blood Test), and a single service is performed across many different appointments. This Many-to-Many relationship is resolved by the Appointment_Service entity.
